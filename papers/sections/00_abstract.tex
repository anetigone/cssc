\begin{abstract}
Large language models can generate formally checkable proofs, but strong
performance often relies on large test-time budgets: many samples, repeated
verifier calls, long contexts, or expensive model invocations. Existing proof
agents usually treat the budget as a stopping condition rather than a signal
that should shape search. We introduce \system, a budget-aware controller for
LLM-guided theorem proving in Lean 4. The controller allocates model, token,
verifier, retrieval, and wall-clock resources across proof generation, local
repair, retrieval, capability tests, decomposition, representation changes,
and model escalation. Its key substrate is a verifier-grounded structured
state in which proof obligations form AND dependencies, alternative proof
branches form OR choices, and every accepted fact is versioned and linked to
checker evidence. This state makes it possible to estimate not only immediate
success, but also residual proof progress, downstream unlock value, and the
information gained by an action. We derive conditions under which cheap search
should precede expensive generation and show that success probability per unit
cost is the optimal ordering rule in a simple sequential model. We evaluate
against direct generation, parallel sampling, fixed escalation, iterative
refinement, and budget-agnostic structured search under matched budgets.
